# Meters naming and cross-cutting attribute conventions

Every Edict `Meter` instrument name lives in `SemanticConventions.{Category}.Meters.{Name}` and follows OpenTelemetry semconv suffix rules: `.count` for counters, `.duration` for time-spent histograms (seconds), `.size` for byte sizes, `.age` for elapsed-since-event measurements, `.lag` for messaging-style end-to-end delay. Compound nouns are dot-nested (`edict.outbox.oldest_entry.age`, `edict.claim_check.payload.size`) rather than underscore-joined; the dotted segments compose into a navigable namespace dashboards can group on. Categories that already exist in `SemanticConventions` (`Commands`, `Events`, `ClaimCheck`) grow a `Meters` sub-class alongside their existing `Spans` / `Tags`; four new top-level categories (`Outbox`, `DeadLetter`, `Sagas`, `Idempotency`) land for the metrics that don't have an existing span home, matching the CONTEXT.md domain taxonomy. Cross-cutting attribute keys reused across multiple metrics — `edict.grain.type`, `edict.command.type`, `edict.event.type` — live under a new `SemanticConventions.Common.Tags` category, not duplicated per-metric. Per-metric attributes that don't generalise (`edict.outbox.effect_kind`, `edict.dead_letter.failure_reason`) stay scoped to their owning category. The single `Meter` is `EdictDiagnostics.Meter` with source name `"Edict"` (mirror of the existing `ActivitySource`); consumers register it with `meterBuilder.AddMeter(EdictDiagnostics.SourceName)` exactly as they do for traces.

The shipped sketch in PRD #172 used names like `edict.outbox.depth` and `edict.event.stream_lag` that read naturally in prose but diverge from semconv ("depth" → "count" of a current state; "stream_lag" carries the substrate noun that's implicit from the metric's parent category). Aligning to semconv suffixes means dashboard authors who know `http.server.request.duration` and `db.client.connection.count` recognise `edict.event.handle.duration` and `edict.outbox.pending.count` without consulting docs — the convention pulls its weight by being the convention. Cross-cutting attributes follow the OTel pattern where `server.address` is one key reused across `http.*` and `db.*` metrics rather than `http.server.address` / `db.server.address`; for Edict that means a single `edict.grain.type` key carries through the outbox, saga and idempotency metrics, and a PromQL filter `{edict_grain_type="OrderCommandHandler"}` works uniformly. The `SemanticConventions.Common` category is the first cross-cutting bucket the convention introduces and is expected to grow as later PRDs land more reusable attributes; it explicitly does not become a dumping ground (any attribute that lives there must appear on instruments in at least two categories). The future-PR rule the convention establishes: when adding a new metric, pick the suffix from this ADR's list, place the constant in the category that owns the metric's domain, and reach into `Common.Tags` only for attributes already shared by ≥2 categories.

## Considered Options

- **Keep the PRD's original sketched names** (`edict.outbox.depth`, `edict.event.stream_lag`, `edict.event.handler_latency`) — rejected. They read well in PRD prose but break the operator's first-time-seeing-this-metric experience; dashboard authors expect the suffix to tell them the instrument shape. Aligning to semconv gives that for free.
- **Underscore-join compound nouns** (`edict.outbox.oldest_entry_age` rather than `edict.outbox.oldest_entry.age`) — rejected. Dot-nesting lets dashboards group on `edict.outbox.oldest_entry.*` if a sibling instrument lands later; underscore-joining collapses the noun into an opaque blob.
- **Per-category attribute keys** (`edict.outbox.grain.type`, `edict.saga.grain.type`, `edict.idempotency.grain.type`) — rejected. Reusing `edict.grain.type` across metrics matches OTel cross-cutting convention and lets one PromQL filter target a grain type across every metric it appears on. The per-category form would force operators to enumerate the prefix per instrument.
- **Bake `Meters` into the existing `Spans` / `Tags` triad without a new `Common` category** — rejected. Cross-cutting attributes have no natural home in any one category; forcing them in (`Commands.Tags.GrainType`? `Outbox.Tags.GrainType`?) misrepresents what they are. The `Common` category is the honest shape.
- **Drop `edict.dead_letter.promotion_age` (a PRD-sketched instrument)** — accepted into this ADR's scope. The sketch carried the instrument but no user story justified it; the slice ships without it. Re-introducing later is non-breaking.
- **Ship `edict.outbox.pending.max` alongside `edict.outbox.pending.count`** — rejected. The "worst-stuck aggregate" alerting story is already served by `edict.outbox.oldest_entry.age` (a longer-stuck entry implies a more-stuck aggregate); shipping two pending-count variants doubles the surface for the same operator concern. PromQL `max()` over the per-silo `pending.count` series gives the cross-fleet maximum without a second instrument.
